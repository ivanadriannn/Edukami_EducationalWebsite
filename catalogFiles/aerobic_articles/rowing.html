Rowing

Rowing is a sport or activity using a rowboat or kayak to move on water by using physical force and rowing motion. In rowing, participants use oars or paddles to propel the boat through the water, propelling the boat forward.

Recommended

~30 minutes of Rowing
Four to six times every week

Benefits
  • strengthen your upper body
  • Strengthen Legs
  • can be meditative
  • Great for your heart and lungs
  • builds power and endurance
  • Burns calories
  • Lower risk of cardiovascular and respiratory disease

How to Do

1. Start by holding the oar with arms extended, knees bent, and weight on the balls of your feet.
2. With your back straight and core engaged, push back using only your legs, rolling through your feet so they’re flat when your legs are extended. Keep your arms extended throughout.
3. After you’ve gotten used to pushing with your lower body, practice arm isolations. With legs straight, pull the oar toward your chest. Bend your elbows out to the sides and touch the oar just under your chest.
