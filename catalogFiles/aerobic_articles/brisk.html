Brisk Walking

Brisk walking is a form of brisk walking at a higher intensity than walking. In brisk walking, a person walks at a faster and more energetic pace, increasing heart rate and breathing rate. Brisk walking is a form of light to moderate aerobic exercise.

Recommended

~30 minutes of Brisk Walking
Every day of the week

Benefits
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Strengthen Legs
  • Improve cardiovascular fitness
  • Strengthen your bones
  • Improve muscle endurance
  • Increase energy levels
  • Lower risk of cardiovascular disease

How to Do

1. Target heart Rate, A safe target heart rate while exercising, for most adults, is 50 to 85 percent of your maximum heart rate.
2. Measure your pace, if you can walk at least 100 steps per minute, you’re walking fast enough to reap substantial fitness benefits.
3. Keep your head up, looking forward, not down. Relax your neck, shoulders, and back, but don’t slouch or lean forward.
