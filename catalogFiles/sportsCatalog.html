Aerobic
Aerobic refers to an exercise or activity that requires an increased intake of oxygen to meet the body's energy demands. It is often referred to as cardiovascular exercise or cardio because it primarily targets the cardiovascular system, which includes the heart, blood vessels, and lungs. During aerobic activities, the body utilizes oxygen to convert stored energy sources, such as carbohydrates and fats, into usable energy. This sustained oxygen consumption enables the body to perform repetitive movements or exercises over an extended period without fatigue.
