Steak

Pan seared steak is a method of cooking steak by frying or quickly frying it on a hot surface, such as a cast iron skillet or non-stick skillet heated with oil or butter. The goal of pan searing is to create a crispy, caramelized outer crust on the surface of the steak, while the interior remains tender and juicy.

Ingredients
  • Steak 12-oz or 6-Oz
  • Kosher salt 1 Teaspoon
  • Ground black pepper Â½ teaspoon
  • Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
  • Unsalted butter 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh thyme leaves few sprigs

Steps

1. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels. Season the steaks all over with the salt and pepper.
2. Turn on your exhaust fan and heat a heavy pan (preferably cast iron or stainless steel) over medium-high heat until it's VERY hot.
3. Add the oil to the pan and heat until it begins to shimmer and move fluidly around the pan.
